How Can the Pesticide Risk Tool Help You Assess Pesticide Risk?

Why Do We Need Risk Assessment Tools?

Pesticide products vary in level of risk.

  • Danger, Warning, Caution signal words; acute mammalian toxicity
  • US EPA Reduced Risk Initiative

Pesticide use sites vary in degrees of vulnerability.

  • Proximity to surface water and other sensitive sites
  • Soil and weather characteristics that affect pesticide movement

Pesticide application methods entail varying degrees of risk.

  • Tree-trunk application vs. air-blast sprayer
  • Foliar application vs. soil incorporation

PRT's Multiple Uses

Metrics

  • Measure risk over time and track improvement
  • Compare pesticide risk within commodities and across varieties
  • Measure the reduction in risks caused by:
    • A reduction in the pesticide application rate
    • Switching to lower-risk pesticide products
    • Changing the timing and/or method of application

Informed Decision-Making

  • Run "what-if" and planning scenarios, use to develop a pest management plan
  • Evaluate risks by supplier, commodity and region
  • Product comparison:
    • Assess relative risks of different products
    • Compare estimated risk to label hazards

History of Risk Assessment Tools

  • Environmental Impact Quotient (EIQ)
    • Developed by J. Kovach, C. Petzoldt, J. Degni and J. Tette
    • Estimates risk to farm workers, consumers and terrestrial organisms
    • Online user interface - runs by pesticide active ingredients
  • Windows Pesticide Screening Tool (Win-PST)
    • Developed by the USDA NRCS
    • Estimates potential for offsite pesticide movement and long-term toxicity to humans and fish
    • Downloadable application runs by pesticide products or active ingredients
  • Pesticide Environmental Assessment System (PEAS)
    • Developed by C. Benbrook
    • Estimates risk to workers, consumers, birds, aquatic organisms, and honeybees
    • Operated by BCS Ecologic
  • Pesticide Risk Management Profiling Tool (PRoMPT)
    • Developed by Unilever Colworth
    • Estimates risk to ground water, terrestrial ecosystems, aquatic ecosystems and operators
    • Online application, runs by pesticide active ingredients

Developed by diverse project team:

Able to run by US EPA registered pesticide products:
